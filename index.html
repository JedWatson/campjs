About CampJS III

CampJS is a Friday to Monday event bringing the full spectrum of JavaScript technology to the widest audience possible. Clientside and serverside. Frameworks and frameworkless. Software and hardware. Beginner to advanced. The goal is for everyone to walk away with more friends, better connections and an upgraded, cutting-edge skillset.

The CampJS Format

CampJS combines the best of both structured and unstructured conferences. Some of the greatest value of a conference is in the people you meet. This opportunity is often limited to the few minutes between talks and the afterparty! This isn't enough time to make real friends! CampJS is basically a weekend-long afterparty, interspersed with optional world-class presentations by invited local & international guests.

We're refining our format based on feedback from prior events. The following outlines what you should expect from CampJS in 2014:

Hack Space

The main space at the venue is primarily reserved for uninterupped hacking. On the Sunday evening people are encouraged to give a short, <3min presentation on what they worked on over the weekend. Prizes and glory will be awarded for the best demos!

The most overwhelming response from the last CampJS survey was the love of our 2 hands-on workshops from CampJS II:

  • Full-stack Node workshop by ThoughtWorks, where participants worked together to hack up a "TRON Game" using JavaScript, some RaspberryPIs and some LED matrices.
  • learnyounode by rvagg. Learnyounode has become an incredibly popular and core curriculum for nodeschool.io.

We are going to try focus on delivering more high quality workshops in 2014. To ensure we create a great learning environment, workshops should be designed for a small group of 5 - 15 participants. Workshops will repeat over the duration of the camp so nobody misses out. Workshops should be run by at least two tutors.

Workshops should be open-source and have enough instruction to be usable for people who aren't attending CampJS. We encourage the use of the Workshopper Framework.

Talks

Talks are limited to fast, 20 minute sessions to encourage more focussed talks. Shallow "Readme" talks are discouraged; talks should be built around original research or personal experiences - something unique! Speakers may opt to run a second session to cover their topic in-depth. Any content which might not fit in a short talk should be run as a workshop!

To reducing the stress and workload of talk preparation and applying some pre-talk sanity checking, we would like to encourage a minimum of two presenters per talk.
